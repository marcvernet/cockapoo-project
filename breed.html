Everything You Need to Know About Cockapoos

From temperament to care, we've got all the details on one of the world's most beloved designer breeds.

🎯 Friendly Nature

Cockapoos are known for their outgoing, friendly personalities. They get along great with children, other pets, and even strangers. Their social nature makes them excellent therapy dogs.

🧠 Highly Intelligent

Thanks to their Poodle heritage, cockapoos are incredibly smart and eager to please. They learn new commands quickly and excel in obedience training and dog sports.

⚡ Playful Energy

While not overly hyperactive, cockapoos have a wonderful playfulness that keeps them engaged and happy. They love fetch, agility courses, and interactive toys.

💕 Affectionate Companions

Cockapoos thrive on human connection. They follow their owners around the house and love cuddling. They can develop separation anxiety if left alone for long periods.

✂️ Grooming Needs

Cockapoo coats can be wavy or curly and require regular brushing 3-4 times a week to prevent matting. Professional grooming every 6-8 weeks is recommended.

🏃 Exercise Requirements

Daily exercise of 30-60 minutes is ideal. This includes walks, playtime in the yard, and mental stimulation through training or puzzle toys.

🦷 Dental Care

Regular teeth brushing 2-3 times weekly helps prevent dental disease. Consider dental chews and regular vet check-ups.

👂 Ear Care

With floppy ears, cockapoos are prone to ear infections. Clean ears weekly and keep hair around ears trimmed for proper airflow.

🏥 Common Health Issues

While generally healthy, cockapoos can be prone to eye problems (cataracts, PRA), ear infections, and hip dysplasia. Regular vet check-ups are important.

💉 Vaccinations

Keep vaccinations up to date including rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and bordetella. Start puppy shots at 6-8 weeks of age.

⚖️ Weight Management

Maintain a healthy weight to prevent joint issues. Monitor food intake and adjust exercise as your cockapoo ages.

👴 Senior Care

As cockapoos age (12+ years), provide joint supplements, softer bedding, and more frequent vet visits to monitor health changes.

🍽️ Feeding Schedule

Puppies: 3-4 meals/day. Adults: 2 meals/day. High-quality small breed dog food is recommended. Always provide fresh water.

📏 Portion Sizes

Teacup: 1/4-1/2 cup twice daily. Toy: 1/3-3/4 cup twice daily. Miniature: 3/4-1 cup twice daily. Standard: 1-1½ cups twice daily.

🚫 Foods to Avoid

Chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol, avocado, and bones are all toxic to dogs. Keep these out of reach at all times.

🦴 Healthy Treats

Use small training treats, baby carrots, blueberries, or commercial dog treats. Limit treats to 10% of daily caloric intake.
